About Us

Many Brooklyn Tech students purchase poor quality fast fashion clothing items because they're usually cheaper and more accessible for an average teenager with little to no income. However, this is an issue as fast fashion exploits the labor of workers, harms the environment, and is produced unethically. In addition, fast fashion, as the name suggests, usually follows quick trends which leads to many people discarding their clothes as soon as the trend fades. So, we decided to create a web application where Brooklyn Tech students are able to sell and buy unwanted clothing items, rather than throwing them away. We aim to make our platform more sustainable than fast fashion retailers such as Shein, Forever 21, and H&M. In addition, since the clothes are being sold second hand, this will solve the issue of affordability.
